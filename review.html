I DO OR I AM DOING?

Review - I do

Do you know the difference?




Language
Focus

I Do

We use the present simple tense when we want to talk about fixed habits or routines - things that don't change.


To affirm something:

I do my homework in the afternoon.

I live with my parents.

I have fun with my friends

To deny something:

I don't do my homework in the afternoon.

She doesn't live with her parents.

I don't have fun with my friends

To ask a question:

Do you do your homework in the afternoon?.

Does she live with her parents?.

Do you have fun with your friends?

Remember

The third person is she, he, and it.

we add an -s to the third person.


In the present simple, we also conjugate with do and does to deny and to ask questions, we don't add- to the verb
